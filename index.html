Turbulence and Energy Systems Laboratory (TESLa)

The Turbulence and Energy Systems Laboratory (TESLa) at the University of Colorado, Boulder studies turbulence, combustion, renewable energy systems, and geophysical flows.

Turbulence Physics and Modeling

At TESLa, we specialize in numerical simulations of turbulent flow problems found in both engineering and nature, with an emphasis on modeling and understanding flow physics.

Turbulent Combustion and Reacting Flows

We perform both fundamental and applied studies of turbulent combustion and reacting flows, with a focus on turbulence-flame interactions and propulsion systems.

Renewable Energy Systems

We study various aspects of wind and ocean renewable energy systems through the use of physically-realistic numerical simulations.

Geophysical Turbulent Flows

Small-scale processes in the ocean are examined to better understand climate and weather modeling, as well as biogeochemical processes in the earth system.

Our Team

The Turbulence and Energy Systems Laboratory (TESLa) is located at the University of Colorado, Boulder and is led by Professor Peter Hamlington in the Department of Mechanical Engineering. The TESLa team currently consists of a number of graduate student researchers and collaborators within Boulder and beyond.
